Financial Formula: Compound Interest

The formula for compound interest is:
\[ A = P(1 + \frac{r}{n})^{nt} \]

Explain the variables: A is the future value, P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate, n is the number of times that interest is compounded per year, and t is the number of years the money is invested for.
